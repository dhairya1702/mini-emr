## Design Context

### Users
Doctors and clinic staff use this interface throughout the workday to manage queue flow, check in patients, move consultations forward, review records, and keep front-desk and clinical operations aligned. The UI needs to support fast scanning under time pressure without feeling frantic.

### Brand Personality
Calm, capable, refined.
The product should feel trustworthy and operationally sharp. It should reduce ambient stress for busy clinics and signal that the system is designed for professional medical environments rather than generic back-office software.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a premium light-mode clinical/editorial direction. Favor warm paper-tinted surfaces, deep ink text, muted botanical or mineral accents, and restrained contrast over bright SaaS blues. The visual language should feel composed, deliberate, and information-dense without becoming cold or sterile. Avoid flashy gradients, dark-mode neon, and generic AI dashboard styling.

### Design Principles
1. Prioritize rapid visual scanning with strong hierarchy, concise labels, and tabular numerics where operationally useful.
2. Keep the mood calm and grounded through warm neutrals, tinted surfaces, and measured contrast instead of bright chrome.
3. Blend editorial typography with utilitarian structure so the interface feels premium without reducing throughput.
4. Make state and urgency visible through layout, spacing, and emphasis rather than decorative noise.
5. Preserve existing workflows and affordances while upgrading clarity, polish, and perceived quality.
